// SCREENPLAY · EPISODE 01

NULL THRESHOLD

"Zero Point"

WRITTEN FOR ANIMATION · EPISODE 1 OF 13
RUNTIME: ~23 MIN
ORIGINAL CONCEPT: NULL THRESHOLD IP
ACT ONE
FADE IN:
EXT. ASHFIELD DISTRICT 7 — ROOFTOP — DAWN

The first image is sky. Not a natural sky — a sky that learned something from a disaster and never forgot it. Deep violet fading into burnt indigo at the horizon. Seven distinct points of impossible light: the Void fractures that ring the Ashfield, their Echo-energy bleeding upward in columns the same color as nothing-good.

The city below: pre-fracture architecture gutted and repopulated with improvised structures. Laundry lines between collapsed towers. Markets in parking structures. A water tower covered in hand-painted district markings. The Ashfield doesn't look like a wasteland. It looks like a place people decided to live in, because the alternative was dying somewhere nicer.
A TITLE CARD burns into the sky in clean monospace typography:

"TEN YEARS AFTER THE FIRST VOID OPENED, THE WORLD REORGANIZED ITSELF AROUND ONE QUESTION: WHO CONTROLS ECHO?"

Beat. A second card: "THE ASHFIELD NEVER GOT TO VOTE."

The title sequence ignites from this — fracture-crack geometry, ECHO ENERGY threading through the show's title: NULL THRESHOLD. The music is not triumphant. It's the kind of music that understands what it's scoring.
INT. VOID FRACTURE V-7A — CONTINUOUS
A world inside a world. The fracture's interior defies the rubble aesthetic of the Ashfield above — inside the Void, geometry has negotiated a truce with Echo energy and arrived at something almost beautiful. Crystal formations the size of cars. Walls that pulse with slow bioluminescent teal. The air is thick with particles of crystallized Echo residue, drifting like underwater snow.

KAIRO VESS moves through this like he works here.

Because he does. He's 19. Patched gear, no corporate insignia, visor cracked and re-sealed three times with the same strip of heavy-duty adhesive that he's going to replace just as soon as — he's never going to replace it. A well-maintained salvage kit on his back. His movements are completely calm: measuring, assessing, occasionally adjusting course by three degrees as if following navigation that only he can see. Because he can.
KAIRO (V.O.)
(dry, internal)
The average Void Diver lasts six months. Before either the fracture kills them or the payment structure kills them — contracts designed specifically to keep Divers one job away from financial survival. Most quit. The ones who don't quit usually die trying to push too deep, too fast, trying to make the math work.
He chips a crystal formation loose with practiced efficiency. Checks the mass. Calculates the grade. Pockets it.
KAIRO (V.O.)
I've been doing this for three years. I'm not brave. I'm just very good at math.
SOUND: A deep, rhythmic pulse from deeper in the fracture. Something large. Something territorial. Kairo doesn't look toward the sound — he counts the rhythm, adjusts his route by four degrees, and keeps moving. His avoidance of the entity is so precise it looks choreographed. It was, essentially. He mapped the patrol cycle six months ago and updates the model daily.
CLOSE ON: KAIRO'S EYES THROUGH HIS VISOR
A subtle visual language the show will use throughout — when Kairo's Probability Cascade activates, the audience sees what he sees: a translucent overlay of probable near-futures, branching like fracture lines from each major physical object. Not a CGI extravaganza. Intimate. A few degrees of visual distortion that communicates he sees something we don't.
KAIRO (V.O.)
Five seconds. That's what I get. Five seconds of probable future for any physical system I'm actively observing. The entity will turn left at the third crystal column. The southeast cluster will support my weight for another eight minutes before the floor cavity beneath it fails. The exit corridor is clear for the next four minutes and twenty seconds.
KAIRO (V.O.)
(beat)
Null, they called it. Three tests at three different facilities. No Echo resonance detected. Nothing. Just a kid from the Ashfield who somehow keeps not dying.
KAIRO (V.O.)
I've been thinking about that lately.
INT. VOID FRACTURE V-7A — MOMENTS LATER
His comms unit crackles — old hardware, analog frequencies, deliberately not on any corporate network. A woman's voice: MARDA, 50s, the kind of voice that has given bad news enough times to have gotten very efficient at it.
MARDA (THROUGH COMMS)
(no preamble)
Kairo. Abort your dive. Right now.
KAIRO
(not stopping)
I have eight minutes of safe window left, Marda.
MARDA
V-9C just went critical. The monitoring net picked it up twenty minutes ago — I've been trying to reach you since. Kairo... it's not pulling. The fracture is pushing.
Kairo stops. In three years, he has not stopped mid-dive for anything. He stands completely still, holding the crystal he just chipped loose, and runs the implications. They do not resolve into anything good.
KAIRO
How long has it been inverting?
MARDA
Best estimate... six hours.
KAIRO
Has Meridian evacuated the district?
MARDA
(silence that says everything)
They sealed the perimeter. Corporate tape. A couple drones. They're waiting for their Apex asset.
Another silence. Kairo puts the crystal carefully into his bag. Zips it closed. Turns toward the exit — and then past the exit, toward the direction of Void 9C.
MARDA
Don't you dare go into an inverting Void, Kairo Vess.
KAIRO
I'm not going in. I'm just going to look at it.
MARDA
...And then?
KAIRO
(already moving)
And then I'm going to make some decisions.
SCENE BREAK
ACT TWO
EXT. VOID FRACTURE V-9C — PERIMETER — DAY
The cordon around Void 9C. Meridian corporate tape — their institutional yellow-black — stretched around a six-block radius. Automated warning drones patrolling at altitude. Two Meridian response vehicles parked at the main entry point, professional and impassive.

Behind the tape: sixty thousand people who live here. They're watching. Some of them are filming. None of them are being talked to. One of the Meridian corporate Resonants manning the perimeter — AGENCY OFFICER REYES, 30s, badge-lanyard version of a soldier — is having a conversation on a private comm line, turned away from the crowd. The body language of someone who has filed away his discomfort and is waiting for end-of-shift.
Kairo arrives at the perimeter tape. He studies Void 9C from here. The inversion is visible even at this distance — the fracture's light moves outward in pulses instead of inward. Like a wound breathing wrong.

His probability overlay activates. He's never seen an inverting Void before. The probability trees it generates are new geometry: instead of the fracture's normal draw-and-contain logic, every branch paths outward.
KAIRO (V.O.)
The inversion creates a pressure system. Echo-contaminated entities — everything inside the fracture that has absorbed enough energy to change — gets pushed toward the surface. Normally the fracture's pull keeps them deep. Without that pull, with active pressure pushing out... in approximately ninety minutes, the inversion will complete. Everything inside the fracture comes out at once. Into the Ashfield. Into sixty thousand people who can't fight back because the world decided they didn't matter enough to register.
He looks at the corporate Resonants standing at the perimeter. He looks at the empty fracture entrance, where no one is going. He looks at the Ashfield residents watching, waiting, holding the hope that something official will happen.

He looks at the fracture.

He does the math.
KAIRO
(quietly, to himself)
The source of the inversion has to be inside. If you reach it and neutralize it, the fracture restabilizes. That's the mechanism. That's the math.
KAIRO (V.O.)
I'm a null-class civilian Void Diver with a salvage kit and six months of Echo crystal worth of savings. This is not my problem. The math absolutely does not make this my problem.
He steps over the tape and walks toward the fracture entrance.
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
(turning, seeing him)
Hey — hey! That's a restricted — you don't have clearance!
KAIRO
(not stopping, calm)
I'm a registered diver. Null class. I'm not a threat to your Void. I'm going to look at it.
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
You'll be dead in thirty seconds in there—
KAIRO
(over his shoulder, sincere)
Thirty-seven, actually. I appreciate the concern though.
INT. VOID FRACTURE V-9C — CONTINUOUS
The inside of an inverting Void is different from anything Kairo has experienced. The Echo-energy moves wrong. The light patterns are inverted. The crystal formations have fractured under the pressure stress and float in the air in slow, dangerous suspension. The entities here — medium-class Echo-adapted creatures, territorial, confused by the wrong-direction pressure — move in erratic patterns Kairo has to recalibrate for in real time.

He does.

He maps the entity movements, the structural stress points, the pressure-current vectors. He finds the path through. He moves through it like water finding the route downhill. Not graceful — precise.
KAIRO (V.O.)
Six hours. I have six hours of inversion data to map before I find the source. The good news: whatever triggered this didn't run away. It's deep in the fracture and it's staying there. The bad news: three teams of Beta Resonants went in before me and none of them came back. Which means whatever is deep in here is operating on a different threat level than a pressure headache and some confused entities.
KAIRO (V.O.)
(beat)
Good news: there's a second anomaly deeper in. Something that doesn't fit the inversion pattern. Something quiet — almost calm — in a way that only happens when a thing isn't being disturbed by the pressure because the pressure knows better than to disturb it. I don't know what that is. I should probably find out before Meridian's Apex asset arrives and does something dramatic about it.
He opens his protein bar. Takes a bite. Keeps moving.
SCENE BREAK
EXT. VOID FRACTURE V-9C — PERIMETER — DAY — LATER
SEYA CROSS lands.

The shockwave from her Kinetic-amplified landing propagates through the pavement in a perfect circle. She straightens from the impact crouch without ceremony, and the Meridian officers at the perimeter immediately find reasons to be looking slightly elsewhere. The casual power-differential of someone who outclasses every person present by several orders of magnitude — and who is very tired of being the most powerful person in every room.
She takes in the scene in one sweep. The empty fracture entrance. The officers at the line. The Ashfield residents behind the extended cordon, watching her with the expression of people who are hoping and who are used to being disappointed by that hope.
SEYA
Where are the response teams?
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
Three Beta-class Kinetic units entered at intervals over the past five hours. No response after the fourth-minute mark for any of them. We flagged for Apex support and held position pending your arrival.
SEYA
You've been holding position for — how long since the first team went in?
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
The fracture inverted six hours ago. First team deployed four hours ago.
Seya looks at the fracture. Looks at the crowd behind the cordon. Looks at the officers standing at the line, not going in.
SEYA
(very quiet)
You sealed the perimeter six hours ago. You didn't tell the residents what was happening. You sent in eighteen people and when they went silent you waited at the line for four hours. For me.
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
Protocol dictates that Apex-class assets—
SEYA
I know the protocol. I'm in the protocol. I'm talking about the sixty thousand people in this district who have been breathing inverted-Void air and watching entities push through the surface seam for six hours while your protocol waited for me.
She walks toward the fracture entrance. Reyes moves to intercept.
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
Cross. Command says full briefing before—
SEYA
(not slowing)
Brief me while I walk. I have approximately eighty minutes before this fracture fully inverts and your protocol has a much worse afternoon.
She steps over the tape and enters the fracture. Reyes watches her go. A beat.
REYES
(to no one)
She never waits for the briefing.
SCENE BREAK
INT. VOID FRACTURE V-9C — DEPTH LEVEL 2 — CONTINUOUS
Seya moves through the fracture fast. Kinetic amplification as a constant field — burning through the inversion headwind, cutting time. Each step costs her. The Meridian monitors along her jaw pulse with data she can feel in her bones. She doesn't slow down.

She clears a corridor of medium-class entities with three moves. Not sadistic, not dramatic — efficient. Minimum force applied at optimal vectors. She was designed for this. She was literally designed for this.

She stops when she reaches a figure sitting on a crystal formation, eating a protein bar.
KAIRO VESS. He looks at her the way you look at someone who arrives exactly when your math said they would.
KAIRO
You're Seya Cross. The Apex Catalyst.
SEYA
(processing what she's seeing)
You're inside an inverting Void fracture eating a protein bar. You're null class. You don't have Echo augmentation. How are you alive right now?
KAIRO
Good question. I've been in here since the inversion started — about six hours. I've been mapping the entity displacement patterns and the inversion velocity. I have a fairly complete picture of the fracture's current state.
SEYA
...For six hours.
KAIRO
I figured someone was going to need the data when they got here. The previous three teams didn't have it. That's probably why they didn't come back.
He stands. Tucks away the bar. Pulls out a worn notebook covered in diagrammatic shorthand.
KAIRO
Eleven entities currently displaced from depth sectors three and four. I know their positions and movement patterns. Inversion source is at depth sector five — I haven't gone that deep yet because the entity density increases significantly and I don't have your abilities. You have a window of approximately eighty minutes before full inversion. The source is deep but stationary, which suggests it's not an entity. It's something that was placed there.
KAIRO (CONT.)
There's also a second anomaly. Something quiet, at depth sector four. Something the inversion pressure isn't touching. I don't know what it is. I couldn't resolve a probability reading on it at all.
A beat. Seya is looking at him with an expression she doesn't immediately have a label for. She has met thousands of Resonants. She has never encountered someone who talked about an inverting Void like a logistics problem to be solved.
SEYA
Why are you here? You're null class. A civilian. This isn't your job.
KAIRO
(simply)
Nobody else was going to be.
She holds his gaze for a moment. Something shifts. She looks at the path ahead.
SEYA
Depth sector five.
KAIRO
Safest route goes through four. Past the anomaly.
SEYA
Lead.
Kairo looks at her — the most powerful Resonant of her generation, telling a null-class diver to lead. He starts moving. She falls in behind him, slightly to his left.

His probability overlay shows him: the precise position in her wake with the lowest resistance path. She'll figure out he found it. Eventually.
SCENE BREAK
ACT THREE
INT. VOID FRACTURE V-9C — DEPTH SECTOR 4 — CONTINUOUS
They move through depth sector four together. Kairo navigating, Seya providing the force to clear what navigation can't avoid. They've found a rhythm in under three minutes — which neither of them comments on.

The sector opens into a chamber: wider than anything they've seen, saturated with inverted Echo energy to the point where the light moves like liquid, ceiling to floor. Suspended crystal formations create a vertical landscape that shouldn't be stable but is.

On the floor: a figure.

Small. Cross-legged. Completely still. At first glance — a child, maybe, or a small teenager. Absolutely no Echo-protection gear. No Resonant augmentation visible. Just: there. In the middle of depth sector four of an inverting Void, calm as someone waiting for a bus.
SEYA
(stopping)
Is that a—
KAIRO
(very still, probability window showing him nothing)
Person. Yes.
ORIN opens their eyes.

They look at Kairo and Seya with an expression of calm interest — not surprise. Not fear. Like people arriving was a thing they expected might happen eventually.

Their eyes, in the Echo-saturated light, hold the faintest amber iridescence. The inversion pressure, which has been affecting everything in this fracture, bends around them like water around a stone.
ORIN
(quiet, slightly formal, like someone who hasn't spoken aloud in a while)
The loud part came back. It does that sometimes.
KAIRO
The inversion?
ORIN
The loud part. It pushed everything up. That's bad for outside, I know. I was going to do something about it but I didn't know where the broken part was exactly.
SEYA
(looking at Kairo)
How long have they been in here?
KAIRO
(running the estimation, quietly)
The Echo saturation levels on their skin... the biological integration patterns... I'd estimate... two years, maybe more.
A long beat. Seya and Kairo look at this person who has been living inside a Void fracture for two years. Orin looks at them without self-pity, without distress. With the practical attention of someone who is trying to figure out the social rules of a situation they haven't been in for a while.
ORIN
You didn't ask what I can do.
KAIRO
We can get to that. Right now we have about seventy minutes before a full inversion event. Do you know where the inversion source is?
ORIN
Yes. I'll show you.
They stand. In the Echo-light, their eyes hold something Kairo's probability overlay genuinely cannot read. He files this.

Later, in a different episode, he'll realize what that means. For now, they have seventy minutes and a Void to close.
INT. VOID FRACTURE V-9C — DEPTH SECTOR 5 — LATER
The source of the inversion: a device. Not an entity, not a natural formation. A device — corporate manufacturing markings, deliberately obscured, but Kairo catches a partial logo and catalogs it. Meridian Corp, sub-division unknown, installation type: inversion trigger.

Someone placed this here intentionally. Someone with access to Void sector five. Someone who wanted this district's fracture to invert.
KAIRO
(low, looking at the device)
This was placed here. This isn't a natural inversion. Someone made this happen.
SEYA
(jaw tight)
I can see the manufacturer markings.
KAIRO
So can I.
They look at each other. The full implication: Meridian Corp, or someone with access to Meridian Corp resources, deliberately triggered the inversion of an Ashfield Void. And then Meridian Corp sent teams in. And then Meridian Corp stood at the perimeter and waited.
SEYA
(making a decision)
I can neutralize it. It'll burn fast and loud. I want you both at the exit before I do.
KAIRO
At your current burn rate, neutralizing a device this dense will cost you—
SEYA
(cutting him off, not unkindly)
I know what it costs. I know how to count. Get to the exit.
A beat. He looks at her. She looks at him. Two people who have been independently choosing to be here when nobody asked them to — recognizing each other.
KAIRO
Thirty seconds after exit. Not forty. Thirty. The pressure cascade from your output will hit the ceiling here at thirty-three.
SEYA
(and for a half-second, something like a smile)
Thirty seconds.
EXT. VOID FRACTURE V-9C — CONTINUOUS
Kairo and Orin emerge from the fracture entrance at a pace slightly faster than walking. At the cordon, Reyes and the officers straighten — and then go very still when they see Orin. A person who has been in a live Void for two years. Not Resonant-class. Not registered. Just: Orin.

Kairo stops at the cordon. Doesn't cross it yet. Watches the fracture entrance.

Twenty-eight seconds after they emerged: the fracture entrance blazes white-gold. An enormous pulse of amplified Echo energy radiates out from the fracture's depth — Seya's output, controlled and specific, burning the inversion device out of existence.

The fracture's light shifts. Slowly, then all at once: from outward to inward. The inversion ends. The Void fracture restabilizes.

At second thirty-one, Seya walks out of the fracture entrance. She's moving normally, no visible damage — but the Meridian monitors along her jaw are pulsing rapidly, logging the cost. She looks like someone who has done something important and paid something real and has already decided it was worth it.
She stops next to Kairo and Orin at the cordon. The three of them look at the restabilized fracture. The Ashfield residents behind the cordon are watching, beginning to understand that the emergency is over, beginning to make the complicated adjustment from fear to something closer to relief.
SEYA
(to Kairo, not looking at him)
Thirty-one seconds.
KAIRO
Close enough.
Reyes approaches, corporate assessment mode fully engaged — and stops when he sees Orin up close. Something in his expression registers recognition, or something adjacent to recognition. A data-point clicking into place that he wasn't expecting.
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
(carefully)
Where did you find them?
KAIRO
Depth sector four. They've been living in there for approximately two years. Who are they?
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
(already reaching for his comms)
I need to make a call before—
Kairo steps between Reyes and Orin. Not dramatically. Just a step.
KAIRO
(quiet)
Who. Are. They.
Reyes looks at him. At Seya. At Orin. At his comms unit. Makes a calculation.
AGENCY OFFICER REYES
(low)
Someone Meridian Corp has been looking for. For eighteen months. Someone who was not supposed to be in that Void.
Orin, beside Kairo, looks at Reyes with their usual level calm. Then looks at Kairo.
ORIN
You didn't ask what I can do.
KAIRO
(a beat, making a decision)
No. And we're going to go somewhere that's not here so you can tell me when you're ready. Come on.
He walks away from the cordon. Away from the Meridian officers. Away from the restabilized fracture. Orin falls in beside him without hesitation.

After three seconds, Seya falls in on his other side.

Reyes watches them go. He makes the call anyway.
EXT. ASHFIELD DISTRICT 7 — ROOFTOP — DUSK
The three of them. The Ashfield rooftop where the episode opened. The same sky — deep violet, seven fractures burning their impossible light. But from here, from these three people, the fractures look like what they are: apertures. Not disasters. Windows.
SEYA
(looking at the fractures)
Someone triggered that inversion deliberately. With Meridian equipment.
KAIRO
Yes.
SEYA
I work for Meridian.
KAIRO
Worked. Past tense, I'd guess, depending on how you handle the report.
Seya looks at him. He's looking at the fractures, not at her — but there's nothing dismissive in it. He's giving her the space to sit with what the word "worked" implies.
ORIN
(looking at the fractures, matter-of-fact)
The inversion wasn't supposed to close. It was supposed to stay open. Whatever they were trying to push out... wasn't the entities.
A beat.
KAIRO
What were they trying to push out?
ORIN
(finally looking at them both)
Me.
Long beat. The fractures pulse. The Ashfield below moves through its evening routines — markets closing, lights coming on, sixty thousand people who are alive right now because three people who had no obligation to be here were here.

Kairo finishes his probability calculation. The branches from this moment extend outward in ways he's never seen before — not because they're chaotic, but because they're vast. Because something about this moment has cracked open a future larger than any he's mapped.
KAIRO
(quiet, to both of them)
I know someone who might be able to tell us what that means. His name is Luca Dren. He used to work for Meridian. He knows where everything is, including the things Meridian doesn't want found.
SEYA
And you trust him?
KAIRO
No. But I trust that his interests line up with ours right now. That's enough to start with.
He looks at them both. Two people he met today. One of them the most powerful Resonant alive. One of them something that doesn't have a classification yet.
KAIRO (V.O.)
I've been doing this math for three years. Probability trees. Optimal paths. Surviving by knowing where the next thing is before it knows where I am. I have never in three years mapped a future this large, this open, this — unresolved. I don't know if I can see five seconds ahead of what's coming.
KAIRO (V.O.)
(beat)
I'm not scared of that. I should be. I'm not. What I am is — interested.
The fractures burn in the violet sky. The Ashfield hums below. The three of them stand in the last light, and the episode closes on something that isn't hope exactly — it's more like the moment before a probability tree opens into something enormous, and you decide to run the calculation anyway.
FADE TO BLACK.
END OF EPISODE ONE
