Cast of Characters

The Resonants

Four people the world already wrote off. One inverted Void. Every answer they find opens three more questions — about Echo, about power, and about what it means to be human in a world that's becoming something else.

// PROTAGONIST · CHARACTER 01

KAIRO VESS

THE HOLLOW
AGE: 19
CLASS: COGNATE
ORIGIN: ASHFIELD DISTRICT 7
STATUS: VOID DIVER · 3 YR

PROFILE

Former ward of the state who grew up in the Ashfield — the collapsed city-district that became a dumping ground for everyone the new world left behind. Tested for Echo resonance three times across three different facilities. Three null results. No power. No awakening. Nothing.

He works as a Void Diver — one of the non-Resonants who physically enters live Void fractures to salvage raw Echo crystals for corporate buyers. The most dangerous job on earth. The average diver lasts six months. Kairo has survived three years through pattern recognition, ruthless operational efficiency, and an almost inhuman tolerance for pain.

The truth: Kairo didn't fail to awaken. His Echo never attached to his biology. It attached to his perception itself — specifically his ability to process cause-and-effect chains in real time. He sees five seconds into the probable future of any physical system he's actively observing. That window grows wider the longer he survives a situation. He is not a fighter. He is a walking inevitability engine.

ABILITY — PROBABILITY CASCADE

Passive Window (5 sec): Always active. Kairo perceives the immediate physical futures of objects, bodies, and forces in his line of sight. Not mind-reading — purely mechanical. He sees where the punch will land before the shoulder completes its rotation.

Deep Read (variable): When Kairo remains in high-stress situations over extended time, his window expands. Documented max in field: 47 seconds of probable-future rendering. Unknown theoretical ceiling.

Limitation: Probability, not certainty. When Orin or Seya act in ways that defy physical prediction patterns, his window becomes unreliable. He's never encountered a problem his perception couldn't parse — until now.

PERSONALITY NOTES

Quietly sardonic. Doesn't perform strength or bravado — has never needed to. Intensely loyal to the few people he trusts, but openly honest that loyalty doesn't mean he'll die for you stupidly. Has a habit of narrating worst-case scenarios aloud in a deadpan tone that either horrifies or bonds people to him instantly. No hero complex. Has a survival complex that occasionally gets mistaken for heroism. Eats protein bars inside death zones. Considers this normal.

// DEUTERAGONIST · CHARACTER 02

SEYA CROSS

THE OVERCLOCK
AGE: 22 · BIOLOGICAL: ~31
CLASS: KINETIC · APEX CATALYST
ORIGIN: MERIDIAN CORP DIVISION 3
STATUS: AWOL · ACTIVE FIELD

PROFILE

The single most powerful Resonant of her generation. Echo-class Apex Catalyst — she amplifies and accelerates any physical process she touches. Speed. Force. Heat. Cellular regeneration. Chemical reactions. She can punch faster than sound, heal a severed limb in thirty seconds, and at maximum output, create localized time dilation around herself.

She should be humanity's greatest weapon. The problem: every amplification she performs costs her lifespan. Not metaphorically. Measurably. Meridian Corp researchers have tracked it in real time. Every major ability use shaves real time off her biological clock. She's 22 and her cellular age reads closer to 31. At her current burn rate, she has approximately four years left.

She knows this. The corporations know this. They use her like a weapon and call it a partnership. When the Ashfield Void inverts and every Resonant team sent in doesn't return, she goes AWOL from her contract to actually do something useful. Nobody sent her. She sent herself. That's always been the problem.

ABILITY — APEX CATALYST

Physical Amplification: Seya accelerates any physical process within her direct contact or short range. Her own body is her primary instrument — speed, force output, regeneration, and thermogenic heat generation.

Cascade Field: At sustained output, her amplification radiates outward, affecting nearby objects and individuals. She can use this to protect others — but every second of Cascade burns three times faster than personal use.

Cost: Lifespan degradation. Tracked in real time by Meridian implants she's never been permitted to remove. She wears her expiry date where others would wear armor.

PERSONALITY NOTES

Explosive where Kairo is still. Solves problems with overwhelming force before she's considered whether force is the right tool — then recalibrates after. Has never been allowed to exist without someone telling her what her power is worth. Furious that someone without any registered power navigates danger better than she does. Privately grateful for it. The arc isn't about softening her — it's about her learning the difference between burning herself up because she was told to and burning herself up because she chose to.

// WILDCARD · CHARACTER 03

ORIN

THE ITERATION
AGE: 17
CLASS: ITERATION · UNKNOWN DEPTH
ORIGIN: UNKNOWN
STATUS: FOUND INSIDE VOID · ACTIVE

PROFILE

Orin was found inside the inverted Void fracture. Not fighting their way out. Not trapped. Living there. Estimated time inside: approximately two years, based on biological markers and Void contamination levels. No one knows how they survived. Orin doesn't remember.

Two years alone in an environment that kills trained Resonants in minutes. The Void adapted to them — or they adapted to it. What emerged is something research classifications weren't built to handle. Orin's file, when Luca eventually pulls it, is largely redacted. The unredacted portions suggest Meridian Corp knew about them for at least eight months before the inversion.

They speak in a slightly detached way — not robotic, but like someone who spent a long time alone and is relearning how to exist around people. They attach to Kairo first because he's the first person who didn't immediately try to test them or figure out what they could do. The loyalty that forms is absolute, without self-preservation, and occasionally terrifying.

ABILITY — ITERATION

Copy Mechanism: Orin can perfectly replicate any physical motion or ability that has been performed on them at least once — not mimicry, not approximation. Full replication at full power, indefinitely. If a monster tears their arm and regenerates it a specific way, Orin gains that regeneration method. If Seya's punch impacts them hard enough to register, Orin can throw that exact punch back.

Accumulated Library: Two years of continuous Void exposure. Hundreds of entities. Hundreds of absorbed traits. Orin doesn't fully know their own capability depth because nothing has pushed them to use everything yet.

Unknown Ceiling: The terrifying implication: the longer Orin is exposed to new abilities, the more their body becomes a compressed archive of things that shouldn't coexist in one organism. What happens when the archive reaches capacity is entirely unknown — and nobody in the group mentions this aloud.

// HANDLER · CHARACTER 04

LUCA DREN

THE ARCHITECT
AGE: 28
CLASS: ARCHITECT · NON-COMBAT
ORIGIN: MERIDIAN CORP RESEARCH (FMR)
STATUS: INDEPENDENT CARTOGRAPHER

PROFILE

Former Meridian Corp researcher who quit when he figured out what corporations were actually doing with Echo data. Now operates as an independent Void Cartographer — mapping fracture behavior, predicting eruption windows, selling data to whoever pays. He doesn't ask what they're using it for. He tells himself this keeps him clean. It doesn't.

His Echo ability is mundane by combat standards but operationally invaluable: structural memory — he can perfectly recall and mentally render any three-dimensional space he's physically been in, with fidelity that doesn't degrade over time. He's been inside more Voids than any living person. He is, in effect, a living atlas of the Void network — which means he also knows things about the fractures that nobody else does. Some of those things he's sharing. Some of them he's sitting on until the price is right.

ABILITY — STRUCTURAL MEMORY

Perfect Spatial Recall: Every three-dimensional space Luca has physically inhabited is stored at full fidelity, permanently. He can render these spaces in his mind and navigate them as if he were still there, even if the physical location has since collapsed or changed.

Cross-Reference: He can overlay multiple spatial maps and identify structural patterns between different Voids — predicting cascade behavior, collapse vectors, and previously undocumented connections between fractures. This is the ability that made him invaluable to Meridian. It's the same ability that showed him what Meridian was doing. He's the only one who knows how deeply the Void network is actually connected.

"I'm the most useful person in any room and the least safe person to trust. I want you to know both things going in."
"Every piece of data has a buyer. I chose this buyer. That's the moral line I'm drawing."
"The fracture didn't invert by accident. Something made it. And whatever made it knew exactly who was inside the Ashfield."
