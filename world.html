World-Building

The Fractured Earth

Ten years after the first Void cracked open, the planet has reorganized itself around a single question: who controls Echo? The answer is never simple, never clean, and never fair.

// CLASSIFIED — MERIDIAN INTERNAL DOC LEAK

What Echo Actually Is

The official Meridian Corp position: Echo is a naturally occurring energy compound produced by quantum-level instability in the Voids. Harvestable. Useful. Commercially viable. A resource. Nothing more.

What the leaked research files suggest: Echo is not a compound. Echo is not energy in any traditional sense. Echo is memory.

Specifically: the residual information-state of a universe that collapsed approximately 4.2 billion years before ours reached its current configuration. A dead universe. Its matter is gone. Its energy dissipated. But its information — every pattern, every structure, every rule it operated by — bled through into the fabric of our spacetime as it died. Echo is what that universe was trying not to forget.

The Voids are not random fractures. They are apertures — specific structural weaknesses in our spacetime where the dead universe's information density is highest. Where it's closest to breaking through entirely.

When Echo attaches to a living host and enables Resonance, it isn't granting a power. It's loading a fragment of the dead universe's operating logic into a biological vessel. The host becomes a carrier of ancient physics that our universe has never experienced.

The Voidborn — those whose frequency has shifted entirely toward Echo — aren't becoming more powerful. They're becoming more that other universe. Which means they are, technically, ceasing to be native to this one.

// POWER CLASSIFICATION

Echo Resonance — The Frequency Model

Unlike hierarchical ranking systems, Resonance is classified by type — the intersection of a person's psychology, physiology, and the Void environment in which they first resonated. Powers aren't assigned; they emerge. Growth happens through trauma integration, not accumulation.

CLASS — 01

Kinetics

Physical amplification — speed, force, regeneration, thermogenic output. The most visible class; Meridian Corp's preferred asset type. Easiest to weaponize. Easiest to understand. Often the fastest to burn out, literally or otherwise.

EXAMPLE: Seya Cross

CLASS — 02

Cognates

Perception and information manipulation. The rarest class because Echo almost never bypasses biology to attach to cognition. Cognates are frequently misclassified as null. Their abilities read as zero on every standard corporate test — which is exactly why the Ashfield is full of them.

EXAMPLE: Kairo Vess

CLASS — 03

Iterations

Adaptive and copy-based ability sets. Theoretically unlimited in scope — their power ceiling is defined only by what they've been exposed to. Currently a class of one documented individual. Meridian Corp's internal classification for Orin is twelve pages of redactions and the note: "Do not engage without authorization."

EXAMPLE: Orin

CLASS — 04

Architects

Spatial, structural, and informational abilities. Not combat-oriented. Meridian Corp undervalued them for years, then realized Void Cartographers were the most operationally dangerous employees in their building — because they knew where everything was, including the things Meridian didn't want found.

EXAMPLE: Luca Dren

CLASS — 05 · CLASSIFIED

Voidborn

Resonants whose Echo frequency has shifted so completely toward the dead universe's operating logic that they are becoming something our universe has no classification for. Voidborn don't level up. They convert. The physical laws they operate under begin to differ from baseline reality.

STATUS: THREAT · SEASON 1 ENDGAME

CLASS — 00

The Null

Those Echo never touched — or those whose Echo attachment was never detected by corporate testing. The Ashfield is full of Null. Kairo is classified Null. Given what Cognate class actually is, the question isn't why the Ashfield has no Resonants. The question is: what happened to all the Cognate-class children who grew up there and tested null?

// WORLD STRUCTURE

Zones of the Fractured World

ZONE TYPE — 01

The Capped Cities

Megacities built around stable Void fractures that corporations harvest for Echo energy. Grid power runs on extracted Echo. The cities are technically safe — anomalies are contained, fractures are monitored, Resonant-class citizens are registered and managed. Functionally authoritarian. Resonants are assets. Non-Resonants are workforce. The difference between citizen and property is written in Echo-class documentation.

ZONE TYPE — 02

The Ashfields

Collapsed zones between cities. The residue of pre-fracture infrastructure, now Echo-contaminated and administratively abandoned. This is where the Null live, where the non-registered live, where anyone the cities couldn't use ended up. The Ashfield has Void fractures too — unstable, unmonitored, and actively dangerous. It also has the only genuine human communities left. Kairo grew up in Ashfield District 7. He knows every crack in every building.

ZONE TYPE — 03

The Deep Voids

Fractures too unstable to harvest, too dangerous to close. Unmapped — or rather, mapped only in Luca Dren's memory. Where the oldest and most adapted Echo-entities exist. Where Orin lived for two years. Where the physical rules begin to shift. Where the truth about the dead universe becomes less theoretical and more immediate. The Deep Voids aren't inside the fractures. The fractures are inside the Deep Voids.

// POWER STRUCTURE

Meridian Corp

The dominant Echo extraction and Resonant management corporation. Meridian doesn't govern — it owns the infrastructure that governance requires. Power, water, data networks in every Capped City run on Meridian's Echo grid. If a government wants to oppose Meridian, they're arguing with the entity that keeps their lights on.

Meridian's villainy is not cartoonish. They don't cackle. They have actuaries. Their decisions are made by people who believe they are making the optimal choices for maximum human flourishing under resource constraints — and they have the data to make a case for it. The fact that their data doesn't count the Ashfields is a footnote in their methodology section.

What Meridian knows about the Voids that they haven't disclosed: the fractures are expanding. Not uniformly — strategically. And the expansion pattern matches something in their Deep Void data that their lead researcher, before he resigned, flagged as "evidence of intent." That researcher was Luca Dren.
