Volume 01 · Chapter 001

Zero Point

"The world already decided you didn't matter. Survive anyway."
// PAGE 01 — FULL SPREAD
ESTABLISHING · FULL BLEED
The sky above Ashfield District 7 is the same color it's been for ten years: deep violet, cracked with light the sun didn't make.
Void fractures. Seven of them visible from this rooftop. The smallest one you could walk through. The largest one has its own name on government maps — not a warning label, just a name. Like a mountain.
Like it belongs.
KAIRO (V.O.): The average Void Diver lasts six months before either the fracture or the paycheck kills them.
KAIRO (V.O.): I've been doing this for three years.
KAIRO (V.O.): I'm not brave. I'm just very good at math.
// PAGE 02
WIDE
Interior of Fracture V-7A. The walls breathe. Echo-light pulses in slow, bioluminescent waves — teal and deep violet, like something underwater and ancient. The floor is crusted with crystallized Echo residue: translucent shards worth three months of Ashfield wages per kilogram.
A figure moves through this with the practiced calm of someone who does their shopping in a place that would kill a normal person.
CLOSE — KAIRO
KAIRO VESS. 19 years old. Patched gear with no corporate insignia. A cracked visor he's fixed three times with the same strip of adhesive. He looks at the crystallized Echo like a contractor looking at load-bearing walls.
KAIRO
(quiet, to himself) Okay. Southeast cluster is 30% denser than the intake report said. Collapse risk on the left column is... eleven minutes, maybe twelve.
// PAGE 03
A low, rhythmic pulse from the Void walls. Something moving in the deeper sections — an Echo-entity, medium class, territorial. Kairo doesn't look at it. He times his movements to its patrol cycle.
KAIRO (V.O.): I can see approximately five seconds ahead. Not the future — probability. The difference matters. The future is certain. Probability gives you room to negotiate.
He chips a crystal loose with practiced, efficient motion. Checks the weight. Pockets it without celebration.
TINK
The entity passes six feet to his right. Massive. Covered in Echo-crystal growths like spines. Kairo is pressed flat against the fracture wall, completely still. He doesn't look scared. He looks like he's doing arithmetic.
KAIRO (V.O.): It'll turn left at the third column. It always turns left at the third column. In seven seconds it'll be out of range. In eight seconds I'll have filled my bag.
// PAGE 04
INSET — COMMS DEVICE
His comms unit crackles. A tinny voice — an Ashfield local, older, named MARDA. She runs the independent diver coordination network out of a collapsed transit hub.
MARDA (COMMS)
Kairo. You need to abort. I mean right now. The V-9C fracture just went critical.
KAIRO
I'm in 7A, Marda. That's two kilometers from 9C.
MARDA (COMMS)
9C isn't pulling. Kairo — it's pushing.
FULL — KAIRO REACTION
A beat of silence. Kairo goes very still. Not because he's scared. Because he's already running through what this means, and none of the probability trees branch anywhere good.
KAIRO (V.O.): An inverting Void fracture pushes Echo-contaminated entities outward instead of containing them. The last time one inverted, they evacuated four districts. It was a Void a fifth the size of 9C. Nobody evacuated the Ashfield last night. Nobody told anyone.
KAIRO
How long has it been inverting?
MARDA (COMMS)
Best estimate... six hours.
// PAGE 05 — TRANSITION
EXTERIOR — ASHFIELD 9C PERIMETER
The perimeter of Void fracture 9C. Corporate security tape — Meridian Corp colors, yellow and black — has been strung around a six-block radius. Automated warning drones circle overhead. Two corporate response vehicles are parked at the entry cordon, engines running.
The Ashfield residents who live in the six-block radius are standing outside their buildings, looking at the tape, looking at the drones, waiting for someone to tell them what to do. Nobody from Meridian is talking to them.
KAIRO (V.O.): Meridian response protocol for an inverting Void in a Capped City: immediate mass evacuation, Resonant containment teams deployed within thirty minutes. Forty-seven casualties maximum in their best-case models. They're proud of forty-seven. Forty-seven is a low number when you're running the math on a city of three million. They ran that math. I've seen it. The Ashfield has sixty thousand people. The math doesn't include us because the math doesn't count us.
Kairo stands at the edge of the cordon tape. His bag is full. He could walk away. He has enough crystal to cover three months of living expenses for every person on his floor. He could walk away.
He doesn't walk away.
// PAGE 06
Kairo crouches, studying the fracture entrance from the perimeter. Void 9C's opening is different — the light inside moves the wrong way, bleeding outward in pulses instead of pulling inward. Two corporate Resonants in full gear stand at the entry. Neither of them goes in.
CORPORATE RESONANT A
Third team hasn't responded in forty minutes.
CORPORATE RESONANT B
Flag it and hold. Command said we wait for the Apex asset.
The "Apex asset" arrives fourteen minutes later. Not in a vehicle. She lands.
SEYA CROSS drops from a height that should break both legs. She absorbs the impact through Kinetic amplification without slowing down, and the shockwave from her landing cracks the pavement in a three-foot radius.
THOOM
She's 22. She looks tired. Not the tired of someone who ran here — the tired of someone who has been tired for a long time and gotten very good at functioning through it.
// PAGE 07
Seya takes in the scene with a single sweeping look. The inactive Resonants. The empty fracture entrance. The Ashfield residents behind the cordon watching her with complicated expressions — hope and resentment occupying the same faces.
SEYA
Three teams in. None out. What class were they?
CORPORATE RESONANT A
Beta-class Kinetics. Six-person standard response units.
SEYA
Eighteen Resonants. Gone in forty minutes. You've been standing here letting this fracture push entities into a residential district for six hours and your response was to send in three Beta teams and then stand at the line.
CORPORATE RESONANT B
Protocol dictates—
SEYA
I don't care.
She starts toward the fracture entrance.
CORPORATE RESONANT A
Cross — command said wait for full briefing. The asset protocol requires—
SEYA
(not stopping) There are sixty thousand people in this district. Brief me while I walk.
// PAGE 08 — KAIRO/SEYA INTERSECTION
WIDE
Seya moves through the fracture entrance. The inverted Echo light pushes back against her like a headwind — she leans into it, Kinetic amplification flaring around her hands and shoulders, burning through it.
She stops. Twenty feet inside the fracture, already past where the previous teams' comm signals went silent, she finds something she did not expect: a figure, sitting on a broken crystal formation, eating a protein bar, looking at the inverted Void walls like they're mildly inconvenient architecture.
Kairo looks up from his bar. He looks at her. He looks at the very visible Meridian Corp asset implants along her jaw and collarbone — the lifespan monitors.
KAIRO
You're the Apex Catalyst. Seya Cross.
SEYA
You're inside an inverting Void fracture eating a protein bar.
KAIRO
I'm a diver. This is technically my workplace.
// PAGE 09
SEYA
You're null. Your class documentation is null. That means you have no Echo resistance, no augmentation, no regeneration. How are you not dead?
KAIRO
Good question. I've been thinking about that.
He stands. Tucks the protein bar away.
KAIRO
I mapped the inversion pulse pattern for the first four hours before you arrived. There are eleven entities currently displaced from deeper in the fracture — I can tell you where each of them is and when each one will cross this corridor. The inversion is accelerating, which means you have a window of approximately ninety minutes before the fracture fully inverts and everything inside gets pushed back out at once.
KAIRO (CONT.)
Good news: whatever triggered this is deeper inside, not at the surface. If you can reach it and neutralize it before the ninety minutes, the inversion stops. Fracture restabilizes.
SEYA
You've been in here for— how long?
KAIRO
Since the inversion started. About six hours.
Seya stares at him. An apex-class Resonant with full Echo augmentation and she's being briefed by someone who tested null, who has been alone inside a critical inversion for six hours, eating a protein bar.
SEYA
...Why?
KAIRO
Because nobody else was going to.
// PAGE 10 — CHAPTER END BEAT
WIDE — DEEP VOID
Two panels. Same visual language, different context.
PANEL A: Seya and Kairo moving deeper into the inverted fracture together. Her amplification flares against the headwind of Echo energy. He walks just behind her, slightly to the left — the precise spot where her wake creates the lowest-resistance path. He found this position in three steps. She hasn't noticed he found it. Yet.
PANEL B: Deep inside the fracture, in a section so saturated with inverted Echo that the light moves like liquid — something crouches on the floor. Small. Still. A person, maybe. Hard to tell from this angle. Hard to tell if they're injured, or sleeping, or completely calm.
KAIRO (V.O.): I've been in a lot of Voids. I have mapped the probability trees of a thousand different environments, a thousand different entities. I have never, not once in three years, encountered a reading I couldn't parse.

KAIRO (V.O.): There is something at the bottom of this fracture.

KAIRO (V.O.): I don't know what it's going to do. I can't see it at all.

KAIRO (V.O.): That should scare me more than it does.
END OF CHAPTER 001

Volume 01 · Chapter 002

Overclock

"She had four years left before she met them. She still has four years. She spent some of it today."
// PAGE 01 — COLD OPEN
ESTABLISHING · MERIDIAN OPERATIONS CENTER
Somewhere that is not the Ashfield. The difference is immediate and total — clean lines, controlled lighting, the architectural language of institutions that believe they will exist forever. A Meridian Corp regional operations center. The kind of building that doesn't have windows facing the Ashfields.
A wall of screens. Fracture monitoring feeds, Echo-extraction telemetry, Resonant asset tracking. One screen shows the stabilized Void 9C. Another shows a biometric signature tagged: CROSS, S. — ASSET ID: AX-07 — STATUS: UNSANCTIONED FIELD DEPLOYMENT.
A man stands at the center console with the stillness of someone who is always in control and has never questioned whether that control is good for anyone besides himself.
VAEL (V.O.): She stabilized the inversion. Good. The Ashfield stays quiet. Better. She found something in sector four that wasn't in the deployment brief. That is a problem.
// PAGE 02
CLOSE — VAEL
VAEL. No last name in any corporate directory — just Vael. 35. The face of someone who has made every calculation about human cost and always gotten the same answer: acceptable. Dark, precise features. Meridian Corp's most senior field Director. Not a Resonant on paper. That paper is wrong.
He studies the biometric data from Seya's monitors. The lifespan readout. The output cost from closing the inversion. His expression doesn't change.
VAEL
She found the Iteration subject.
An aide behind him — young, efficient, already afraid.
AIDE
The subject appears to have exited the fracture with her. With Cross. She's currently in Ashfield District 7 with the subject and an unregistered civilian.
VAEL
The diver. Kairo Vess. Null class. Three-year field record in Ashfield fractures.
AIDE
You know him?
VAEL
I know his test results. Three nulls across three facilities. I ordered the third test myself eighteen months ago.
A beat.
VAEL
Reactivate Officer Reyes at the 9C perimeter. Tell him I want all three under observation — not contact. Observation.
// PAGE 03 — SCENE SHIFT
INT. TRANSIT HUB 14 — LUCA'S WORKSPACE — NIGHT
An abandoned transit hub in Ashfield District 4. The kind of space that's been collapsed long enough to become something else — Luca Dren has been here for two years and the workspace has evolved accordingly. Maps on every wall. Not printed maps — hand-rendered, dense with Luca's annotation system. String connecting fracture locations. Echo-bloom charts. A desk buried in data tablets and one very good coffee setup that represents his last surviving luxury from his corporate life.
He's at the desk when the three of them arrive. He looks at Kairo. He looks at Seya — and his expression registers something quiet and complicated. He looks at Orin and goes very, very still.
LUCA DREN
(to Kairo, not taking his eyes off Orin) You were supposed to be on a salvage dive.
KAIRO
I was. Things escalated.
LUCA
That's — (finally looks at Kairo) — that's an Iteration-class subject. That's the Iteration-class subject. The one on the Meridian restricted manifest that I was never supposed to have access to and definitely never read.
KAIRO
I know.
LUCA
We're in significant danger, Kairo.
KAIRO
I know. That's why I'm here.
// PAGE 04
CLOSE — LUCA
Luca stands. Moves to his map wall. Touches a particular fracture marker — Void 9C — then traces a line to three other fractures in a pattern that suddenly looks deliberate instead of random.
LUCA
The inversion of 9C wasn't isolated. Look at this. Four weeks ago, V-12 in District 2 showed anomalous push-patterns for six hours before restabilizing on its own. Three weeks ago, V-3A in the outer Ashfield registered inversion precursors — they flagged it as equipment error and buried the report. Last week, V-9C.
SEYA
Someone was testing the process.
LUCA
And 9C wasn't a test. It was the real deployment. They wanted it to stay inverted. They wanted what was inside to come out — or to force out whatever was already in sector four.
ORIN — REACTING
Orin, sitting cross-legged on the floor studying the maps like they're familiar furniture, looks up.
ORIN
They've been trying for eight months. The other two times were smaller.
Everyone turns to look at them.
KAIRO
You knew there were previous attempts.
ORIN
The fracture told me. When they put something in the walls, the whole Void changes — the pressure, the sound. I learned the difference between the Void doing what the Void does and the Void doing what someone made it do.
LUCA
(quietly, to himself) The fracture told you.
// PAGE 05 — CHARACTER BEAT
CLOSE — SEYA'S HANDS
While Luca and Kairo talk maps, Seya has moved to a corner of the hub. She's looking at her hands. The Meridian monitors along her jaw pulse at a slightly elevated rate — the residual cost from the inversion closure still registering. She runs a thumb over the monitor line on her collarbone. A habitual gesture. Checking a number she already knows.
SEYA (V.O.): Twelve minutes of sustained Cascade output closing the inversion. Current cellular cost: estimated four months. Not four months off my expected lifespan — four months off what's left. I had forty-eight months left this morning. Now I have forty-four.
SEYA (V.O.): I've been doing that calculation every day for two years. It stopped being terrifying around month six. Now it's just... accounting.
Kairo appears beside her. Not saying anything. He leans on the wall next to her and looks at the same nothing she's looking at. This is, somehow, exactly right.
SEYA
(without looking at him) Don't do the math out loud.
KAIRO
I wasn't going to.
SEYA
People always do the math out loud. Like I haven't already done it. Like they're giving me information.
KAIRO
You closed a critical inversion alone. You're forty-four months ahead of the Ashfield being a disaster zone. Seems like a reasonable trade.
She looks at him. He's not performing comfort. He actually means it as math. She almost laughs.
SEYA
You're a strange person, Kairo Vess.
KAIRO
Probably.
// PAGE 06 — INFORMATION DROP
WIDE — LUCA'S MAP WALL
Luca has pulled a specific map from a locked drawer — not hand-rendered like the others. This one is printed on Meridian Corp stock, marked INTERNAL RESTRICTED. He spreads it on the main table. The group gathers.
The map shows the entire Void network across the Ashfield. But from this angle, with Luca's annotations overlay, the fractures aren't scattered randomly — they form a pattern. A configuration. They connect.
LUCA
I found this eight months before I left Meridian. This is why I left Meridian. The Void fractures aren't random geological events. They're not random anything. They're apertures — specific points in a deliberate structure. The entire Ashfield Void network is one system. One connected system, dormant.
SEYA
Dormant meaning—
LUCA
Meaning it has an active state. The inversions aren't destabilizing the fractures. They're activating them. Someone is warming up the network. Each inversion charges the connected fractures a little more. If all of them fully invert simultaneously—
He doesn't finish. He doesn't need to. The map makes the ending visible.
KAIRO (V.O.): The network activates. The barrier between this universe and whatever is on the other side of the fractures — the dead universe, the source of Echo — opens completely. Not a crack. A door. And something decided it wants that door open.
// PAGE 07
Everyone is looking at the map. Everyone except Orin, who is looking at the wall — at a specific point on the wall that corresponds to a position on the map. Their expression is the most complicated it's been since they emerged from the Void.
ORIN
There's something in the network. Not an entity. Something older. It's been in the deep Void longer than the fractures have been open. I heard it sometimes, in the dark. It's not dangerous.
ORIN (CONT.)
It's not dangerous to things that belong here. It is what Meridian is trying to reach. And it doesn't want to be reached by them.
KAIRO
How do you know what it wants?
ORIN
(simply) It told me. In the dark. That's why they put the trigger in 9C. They wanted the inversion pressure to push me out — away from it. So I couldn't hear it anymore.
A long silence. Luca is writing. Seya is staring at Orin. Kairo is running probability trees on the new information — and finding, for the second time in one day, that his window won't resolve a clear answer.
LUCA
(not looking up from his notes) The thing in the deep network. Did it tell you anything else?
ORIN
It said there's a person who can read the network. Someone who has always been able to read it, who was told they couldn't. It said that person is the reason the door was never supposed to be opened by force — because if the right person opens it instead, what comes through is different.
Nobody says Kairo's name. They all think it.
// PAGE 08 — OUTSIDE THREAT
EXT. TRANSIT HUB 14 — SAME TIME
The street outside the hub. Officer Reyes stands in the shadow of a collapsed transit arch, watching the building. He's off his corporate uniform — civilian clothes, nothing that marks Meridian. His comms earpiece is active.
REYES (LOW)
All three subjects are inside. The cartographer's location. I have eyes on ingress.
VAEL (EARPIECE)
Maintain distance. Do not enter. The Cross asset is unpredictable off-contract and the Iteration subject's threat classification is unverified at close range.
REYES
The null. Vess. He looked right at where I was standing when he came out. He knew I was here before I — how did he know?
VAEL (EARPIECE)
(a pause that contains information) That's a good question, Reyes. Stay on observation.
REYES — INTERNAL
Reyes watches the building. He has the look of someone doing their job and being quietly uncomfortable about it — not enough to stop, just enough to notice.
REYES (V.O.): I've been with Meridian Field Operations for six years. I have watched them seal perimeters in four Ashfields and three outer districts. I have given the order to hold the line and wait for Apex assets while people inside the perimeter didn't get evacuated. I filed the reports. I used the language they gave me. "Operational constraint." "Resource allocation." "Acceptable exposure window."
REYES (V.O.): Today a null-class diver walked into a critical inversion because nobody else was going in. I've been thinking about that for six hours and I haven't found a comfortable place to put it.
// PAGE 09 — ANTAGONIST REVEAL BEAT
INT. MERIDIAN OPS — VAEL — ALONE
The operations center after the aide has left. Vael alone with the screens. He pulls up a file — not on the main display, on a personal tablet that isn't connected to the corporate network. The file is labeled with a designation that shouldn't exist in any Meridian system:
PROJECT THRESHOLD — ORIGIN DOOR — RESONANT SUBJECT: NULL/COGNATE — STATUS: UNCONTROLLED
Below it: Kairo Vess. His test results from all three facilities. But this version of the file has annotations that the official version doesn't have. Notes in Vael's handwriting — crisp, clinical, certain.
One line, circled: "Echo attachment confirmed — cognitive substrate, not biological. Standard tests measure biological. He will never register. He will always be exactly what we need."
Vael's face in the screen glow. The tablet light catches something at the base of his neck — a mark, partially hidden by his collar. Not a Meridian implant. Older. A geometric pattern worked into skin, the same amber-iridescent tone as Orin's Echo absorption marks.
He's not unregistered. He's misregistered. Deliberately.
VAEL (V.O.): Cross will refuse to return to contract. That's acceptable — I need her off the board for what comes next, not on it. The Iteration subject is the variable. Whatever it heard in the network changes the equation. And Kairo Vess...
The door unlocks whether he wants it to or not.
VAEL (V.O.): The question is whether I get to choose what walks through.
// PAGE 10 — CHAPTER END BEAT
WIDE — TRANSIT HUB 14 INTERIOR — LATE NIGHT
The four of them. Luca has made coffee. This is, somehow, the most human moment of the chapter — four people sitting around a workspace covered in fracture maps, holding mugs, in the quiet after the information has settled.
Orin holds their mug with both hands and stares into it. Coffee is, apparently, new. They take a careful sip and their expression does something small and genuine — surprised pleasure. Kairo watches this and his probability overlay, for once, just shows him the next few seconds: this moment, continuing, without threat.
Seya is looking at the map. Not the network — the individual fractures. The ones over the Ashfield districts. The people the map doesn't show, under every one of those marks.
SEYA
If the network activates — full inversion across all connected fractures — how many Ashfield residents are in the exposure zone?
LUCA
(honest) All of them. Every unregistered district within the fracture network. Approximately two hundred thousand people.
Silence.
SEYA
Then we stop it.
LUCA
That's a very clean sentence for an extremely complicated problem.
SEYA
I know. I'm good at clean sentences. Kairo's good at the complicated part.
She looks at Kairo. He's looking at the map — at the network configuration, at the pattern Luca annotated, running it through his probability window. The branches are vast. They branch from here in ways he's never mapped before. But for the first time, they don't dead-end.
KAIRO (V.O.): I have mapped a thousand fractures. I have never been able to map what comes next. Looking at this room — these three people, this table, these maps — I can see forward further than I ever have. Not because the future is clearer. Because for the first time, there are people in the frame. You can see further when you're not alone in the calculation.

KAIRO (V.O.): Four people the world wrote off. One network someone wants to weaponize. Two hundred thousand lives in the Ashfield that nobody in a Capped City is counting. And whatever is at the center of that network, waiting, which told Orin the door can be opened the right way.

KAIRO (V.O.): I'm still not brave. I'm just very good at math. And the math says we have a window.
END OF CHAPTER 002

Volume 01 · Chapter 003

Signal

"When the math stops working, you find out what you actually are."
// PAGE 01 — COLD OPEN · MERIDIAN OPS · 03:00
ESTABLISHING — MERIDIAN OPS · NIGHT
Three hours after midnight. The operations center is skeleton-staffed — only the overnight monitoring team, most of them too junior to understand what they're looking at. Vael is here anyway. He's always here. Nobody has ever seen him leave.
He stands at the main console with his private tablet, running a sequence. Not hiding it — there's nobody here he needs to hide it from. He inputs a command string, and on the largest monitoring screen, Void fracture V-3A in the outer Ashfield begins to pulse differently. Outward. Slow at first. Then faster.
VAEL (V.O.): The network needs six nodes active simultaneously to reach threshold ignition. V-9C was node one. V-3A is node two. Four more after this and the sequence becomes irreversible — the network propagates its own activation without external triggers. I won't need to be in the room for the end. I prefer it that way.
VAEL (V.O.): The group in the Ashfield will respond to this inversion. They'll have to. I'm counting on it. Every step they take toward understanding the network moves them closer to the position I need them in. The null, in particular, is already inside the process — he just doesn't know he's been inside it since the first test.
On the screen: V-3A. Inverting. In the residential corridors above it, lights are coming on. Sixty thousand people in the outer Ashfield starting to understand that something is wrong with the sky again.
Node two. Active.
// PAGE 02 — TRANSIT HUB 14 · 03:14
INT. LUCA'S WORKSPACE
Everyone wakes up differently. Luca was already awake — he doesn't seem to sleep so much as pause. Seya snaps awake instantly, combat-ready, looking for a threat. Orin doesn't appear to have been sleeping at all; they're in the same spot as before, now looking at the ceiling. Kairo opens his eyes and immediately checks his probability overlay, like a sailor checking the horizon.
MARDA is already at the door when it opens — she's been standing outside it for four minutes, deciding whether to knock.
MARDA. 54. Built for endurance not combat — the body of someone who has spent thirty years doing hard physical work in environments that discourage visitors. Her face is the face of someone who has delivered bad news so many times she has learned to deliver it without softening it, because softening it doesn't help anyone.
She runs the independent Void diver coordination network out of a collapsed transit hub. She has been doing this for twenty years. She trained Kairo when he was sixteen. He trusts her more than almost anyone alive, which is why the look on her face when she walks in — not frightened, not urgent, but something older than both of those — lands like a weight.
MARDA
V-3A just inverted. Outer Ashfield. And Kairo — I need to tell you something I should have told you three years ago.
// PAGE 03 — THE CONFESSION
WIDE — ALL FIVE — THE TABLE
Five people around Luca's table now. Marda puts a worn data-chip on the table's surface — old hardware, Meridian manufacture, the kind used in the first year of the fracture network before the technology was updated. The kind from the very beginning.
MARDA
Ten years ago, three weeks after the first fractures opened, I went inside one. Not as a diver — there were no divers yet. I went in because the fracture was three blocks from my building and I wanted to know what it was before I decided whether to run from it. I was in there for four hours. When I came out, I tested positive for Echo resonance. Class unknown — the classification system didn't exist yet. I suppressed the result and I have been suppressing it ever since.
LUCA
(very still) You've been running divers into Void fractures for twenty years. While holding an unclassified resonance.
MARDA
I know the fractures, Luca. Not from maps. From twenty years of people going in and coming out and me tracking every variable. I have things in my head that your wall doesn't have.
She looks at Orin then. For the first time, her composure shifts — a recognition that is something more than recognition. Something like relief.
MARDA
I heard it too. In the first fracture, ten years ago. The thing in the deep network. It told me it was waiting for someone. I've been coordinating divers in the Ashfield ever since because I thought — if I could just keep people alive long enough, eventually the right person would go in and come back with an answer.
KAIRO
(quietly) Marda.
MARDA
Three years, Kairo. I've been watching you not die in fractures you had no business surviving for three years. I know what that is. I knew before you did.
KAIRO (V.O.): I've been running probability trees on Marda for three years and I never once got a clean read on her. I thought it was familiarity. The trees don't work well on people you trust completely. Now I understand: I couldn't read her because she was carrying something she hadn't decided to show anyone yet. The probability of a secret that old, held that tightly, is almost indistinguishable from certainty.
// PAGE 04 — MOBILIZATION
The group moves fast. Luca at his maps, cross-referencing V-3A's position against the network configuration. Seya checking her Meridian monitors — the lifespan readout, running calculations she won't share aloud. Orin watching Marda with the focused attention they give to things they're trying to understand.
LUCA
V-3A is node two. Look — if I'm reading the network configuration right, the activation sequence is systematic. Nodes get activated in order of structural proximity to the network's center point. The center is here.
He points to the map. The center of the network sits directly under Void 9C. Under the Ashfield. Under sixty thousand people.
SEYA
If we stop V-3A the same way we stopped 9C—
LUCA
We buy time. But the network is starting to self-propagate. Each activation charges the adjacent nodes. We stopped the trigger at 9C, but the charge it created is still in the system. At some point stopping the triggers won't matter — the nodes will activate on their own.
ORIN
(matter-of-fact) There are six nodes. We've stopped one. There are five left. If we stop them all before threshold, the network doesn't reach ignition. The Signal told me that. It also said someone has to go to the center and meet it — or the network stays armed indefinitely, even without igniting.
KAIRO
Going to the center means going into deep network. Past where anyone has mapped.
ORIN
Past where anyone but me has been.
MARDA
And me.
Silence. Marda and Orin look at each other — two people who heard the same voice in different decades. Something passes between them that the others can't fully read.
KAIRO
V-3A first. Then we plan the deep run. Let's move.
// PAGE 05 — SABLE · FIRST APPEARANCE
EXT. OUTER ASHFIELD — V-3A PERIMETER — NIGHT
The outer Ashfield at night — less populated than District 7, more raw. The inverted V-3A fracture pulses its wrong light across a landscape of collapsed pre-fracture infrastructure. No Meridian corporate tape yet. Too new. Too fast.
