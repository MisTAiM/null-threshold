Character Design Sheets

Visual design specifications, color guides, and animation notes for the core cast. Production reference document — Season 01.

DESIGN SHEET · DS-001
KAIRO VESS
THE HOLLOW · COGNATE CLASS · NULL (REGISTERED)
AGE: 19 / HEIGHT: 5'10"
BUILD: LEAN · WIRY MUSCLE
HAIR: BLACK · CLOSE-CUT, SLIGHTLY LONGER ON TOP
EYES: DARK BROWN · PROBABILITY OVERLAY: TEAL
SKIN: MEDIUM-DARK · LIGHT VOID SCARRING L. FOREARM

Silhouette Design Brief

Kairo's silhouette is deliberately non-threatening. He should read as ordinary at first glance — the visual contrast to how dangerous he actually is needs to be earned, not telegraphed. Medium height, medium build, no imposing physical presence. His power is invisible. His silhouette should be too.

He moves in a distinctive way: extremely controlled, minimal wasted motion, each step placed with the precision of someone who knows exactly where they're going before they get there. He navigates crowds and environments with a subtle constant awareness that looks like calm and is actually calculation.

Costume — Base Design

  • Patched Void-diver coverall, deep navy with grey-green weathering
  • Cracked visor (fixed with one strip of amber adhesive — it's a character detail, never replace it)
  • No corporate logos. Deliberate — he's worked for six companies and stripped all the branding off everything
  • Worn fingerless gloves, right hand, for Echo-crystal handling
  • Salvage kit backpack: well-maintained inside, battered outside
  • Protein bars in every pocket. This is load-bearing character design.
  • Light void-scarring on left forearm: old, healed, visible when sleeves are rolled

Ability Visual Language

Probability Cascade: When active, Kairo's eyes develop a subtle teal bioluminescence — not dramatic, easy to miss unless you're looking for it. The probability overlay the audience sees should be rendered as translucent branching geometry: thin, precise, like structural engineering drawings over reality. Not magical-looking. Analytical.

Deep Read: As the window expands, the overlay becomes denser. At maximum range (documented 47 sec), the visual language becomes almost overwhelming — but Kairo's expression remains calm. The contrast between internal complexity and external stillness is the visual point.

Color Palette

#1A2035 — Coverall base (deep navy-grey)
#3A4A3A — Weathering overlay (grey-green)
#00FFD4 — Ability / eye overlay (Echo teal)
#D4956A — Skin (medium-dark warm)
#D4A84A — Visor adhesive strip (amber)

EXPRESSION + ANIMATION NOTES

DEFAULT STATE

Slightly narrowed eyes. Not suspicious — processing. The look of someone who's always doing arithmetic on what they're seeing.

CALCULATING

Completely still. Eyes track but body is motionless. This is his most dangerous-looking expression and it looks exactly like someone standing still.

RARE — GENUINE SMILE

Happens twice in season 1. Not a smirk. A real smile, one that reaches the eyes, almost surprised out of him. Save it. Use it sparingly. Weight it.

PROBABILITY BLIND SPOT

When he can't read a probability — first happens with Orin — a micro-expression: eyes widen slightly, a single quick blink, then he recalibrates. Nobody should notice it the first time they see it.

DESIGN SHEET · DS-002
SEYA CROSS
THE OVERCLOCK · KINETIC CLASS · APEX CATALYST
AGE: 22 (BIO: ~31) / HEIGHT: 5'8"
BUILD: ATHLETIC · STRONG · CARRIES TENSION
HAIR: DARK AUBURN CUT CLOSE · FUNCTIONAL
EYES: AMBER-GOLD · BURN RED-WHITE DURING OVERCLOCK
SKIN: WARM BROWN · MERIDIAN IMPLANTS: JAW + COLLARBONE

Silhouette Design Brief

Seya should read as powerful before she does anything. Her silhouette carries weight — broad shoulders, a stillness between actions that communicates contained energy. She is always slightly more present in a frame than the other characters. Not because she's taller or bigger — because of how she occupies space.

Her posture is the posture of someone who learned their body is a weapon and has never quite been able to hold it like it isn't. A constant, subtle readiness. Not a pose — a habit.

Costume — Base Design

  • Meridian Corp asset uniform: dark charcoal with crimson corporate stripe — she hates it, wears it because she has to
  • After going AWOL: stripped the corporate stripe. Same base uniform, no insignia. It's a costume choice that reads as character development.
  • Meridian lifespan monitors: thin luminescent lines along jaw and collarbone, pulsing rhythmically. Always visible. She can't remove them.
  • No jewelry. No personal effects. She's been corporate property for four years. She hasn't had anything to keep.
  • Hands: knuckle scarring from early training, well-healed. She hits things. She always has.

Ability Visual Language

Kinetic Amplification: Manifests as heat distortion around contact points — not glowing energy, not sparks. The visual language of amplified reality: air shimmering, pavement cracking in radial patterns from impact points, speed-blur that conveys genuine physical force rather than anime energy effects.

Cost Visual: The Meridian monitors pulse faster during ability use. After major output, her hands shake — barely visible, controlled. She hates this. She hides it. The audience should catch it before the other characters do.

Color Palette

#1C1F2A — Uniform base (dark charcoal)
#FF2D55 — Meridian stripe / ability (blood red)
#C87941 — Skin (warm brown)
#E8A04A — Eyes (amber-gold default)
#FFFBE8 — Monitor pulse / max output (white-hot)
DESIGN SHEET · DS-003
ORIN
THE ITERATION · ITERATION CLASS · CLASSIFICATION UNKNOWN DEPTH
AGE: 17 / HEIGHT: 5'5"
BUILD: SLIGHT · COMPACT · DECEPTIVELY STRONG
HAIR: WARM BLACK · LONG, LOOSE, PRACTICALLY UN-MAINTAINED
EYES: DEEP BROWN → AMBER IRIDESCENCE IN ECHO-LIGHT
SKIN: LIGHT-MEDIUM · ECHO ABSORPTION PATTERNS: FAINT GOLDEN

Silhouette Design Brief

Orin's silhouette should be the most ordinary of the cast at first glance — and this should feel wrong in context. They're the most powerful and unknown quantity in the group. That dissonance is the point. Small frame. Long hair slightly obscuring the face. Slightly hunched posture from two years of Void-adapted living.

The visual tell that something is different: Orin's stillness. Not Kairo's calculating stillness — a deeper stillness, like an animal that doesn't need to track threats because it has already absorbed all the information about the threats. They are never tense. Even in combat, they are calm. This should be slightly unsettling to look at.

Costume — Base Design

  • Improvised clothing: repurposed pieces salvaged from inside the Void over two years
  • Echo crystal fragments woven into hair — not decorative, they just ended up there and Orin kept them
  • No shoes for first several episodes (bare feet in the Void, Void-hardened). This is both design and characterization.
  • When they acquire regular clothing (Episode 3): immediately covers everything. Not modest — unfamiliar with the social function of showing skin in normal environments.
  • Subtle golden geometric patterns on skin: Echo absorption pathways made visible. Not glowing normally — catch the light in high-Echo environments.

Ability Visual Language

Iteration: When Orin copies and activates an absorbed ability, their eyes shift to full amber iridescence — brighter than the baseline hint. The specific ability manifests with its original visual language (Seya's copied Kinetics = heat distortion; monster regeneration = golden cellular glow). The tell that it's iteration: a half-second of the amber eye-shift before any activation.

Archive Depth: When accessing deeply absorbed abilities (ones they've carried for a long time), there's a cascade effect in the eye iridescence — layered ambers, like looking through amber glass on amber glass. This should feel vast and slightly wrong in a way the audience can't immediately articulate.

Color Palette

#2A1F0D — Void-salvaged cloth (deep warm brown)
#FFB930 — Eye iridescence / absorption marks (amber)
#C49A6A — Skin (light-medium warm)
#00FFD480 — Echo crystal fragments in hair (teal translucent)
DESIGN SHEET · DS-004
LUCA DREN
THE ARCHITECT · ARCHITECT CLASS · NON-COMBAT
AGE: 28 / HEIGHT: 6'0"
BUILD: TALL · LEAN · SCHOLAR NOT SOLDIER
HAIR: DARK GREY-BROWN · PUSHED BACK · ALWAYS SLIGHTLY MESSY
EYES: PALE GREY · DISTANT FOCUS WHEN ACCESSING MEMORY
SKIN: LIGHT WARM OLIVE · PERPETUAL UNDER-SLEEP CIRCLES

Silhouette Design Brief

Luca is the tallest of the group and the most conventionally "professional" — he has history as a corporate researcher and it shows in his bearing. But he's been living independently for two years since leaving Meridian, and the professional polish has weathered. He looks like someone who knows how to wear a suit but hasn't worn one in a while and is fine with that.

Key visual tell: when he accesses his Structural Memory ability, his eyes go distant and his gaze tracks something that isn't there — he's navigating a mental space. This should look slightly dreamlike rather than analytical. His gift operates more like memory than calculation.

Costume — Base Design

  • Field-worn cartographer gear: layered practical clothing, multiple pockets (all of them actually used)
  • Former Meridian Corp researcher badge, still on a lanyard around his neck — not nostalgia, he uses it to access legacy corporate systems he probably still shouldn't have access to
  • Good quality boots, well-maintained — the one thing he kept from his corporate days
  • Wire-rimmed glasses: he doesn't need them for vision correction. They have a Structural Memory overlay display built in — when activated, faint blueprint-blue geometric overlays appear on the lenses
  • Cigarettes. He's trying to quit. He's not succeeding.

Ability Visual Language

Structural Memory: Luca's ability is internal — the external visual tells are subtle by design. When he accesses a mapped space, his eyes go distant, pupils dilate slightly, and the Structural Memory overlay activates in his glasses: thin blueprint-blue lines tracing the architecture of the space he's rendering. The effect is quiet. It should feel like watching someone remember.

Cross-Reference: When he overlays multiple spatial memories simultaneously, he goes very still and sometimes moves his hands — tracing geometry he can see that nobody else can. This is his most overtly "powered" visual behavior and it should look more like a scholar lost in research than a Resonant activating an ability.

Color Palette

#2A2F3D — Field coat (dark slate)
#4A90E2 — Memory overlay (blueprint blue)
#C4AD8A — Skin (warm olive light)
#B8C4D0 — Eyes (pale grey)
#E8C86A — Meridian badge remnant (faded corporate gold)

DESIGN SHEET · DS-005
VAEL
THE DIRECTOR · MERIDIAN FIELD OPS · CLASS: REDACTED
AGE: 35 / HEIGHT: 6'1"
BUILD: LEAN · CONTROLLED · READS AS NON-THREAT
HAIR: BLACK · CLOSE-CUT · ALWAYS PRECISE
EYES: DARK · STILL · ALMOST NEVER BLINK AT NORMAL RATE
SKIN: DEEP BROWN · NECK MARK: AMBER GEOMETRIC — HIDDEN

Silhouette Design Brief

Vael's design principle: he should never read as a villain on first viewing. He should read as the most competent person in any room — which he is. Lean build, precise movement, corporate attire that fits perfectly. The visual language of someone who controls environments rather than reacts to them.

The tell that something is wrong comes in stillness. Vael is too still. Not the stillness of someone calm — the stillness of something that has learned to perform calm so well it forgot to build in the small movements that make humans look human. He blinks slightly less than a normal person. He never fidgets. His hands are always placed deliberately. This should register as unsettling before the audience knows why.

Key design element: The neck mark — an amber-iridescent geometric pattern just above his collar line — should be nearly invisible in normal lighting and catch the light exactly once in Chapter 2 in a way the audience will rewatch.

Costume — Base Design

  • Meridian Corp executive attire — not a uniform, a suit. Charcoal-black, impeccably fitted, no visible Meridian insignia (he's past the level where he needs to wear the brand)
  • No visible tech except a single personal tablet — not corporate-networked. Private. Always on his person.
  • High collar: always worn up or with a jacket that covers the neck mark. This is not accidental.
  • No weapons visible. This is not because he doesn't have them.
  • Shoes that don't make sound on any surface. Again, not accidental.

Color Palette

#111318 — Suit (near-void black)
#6B3A2A — Skin (deep warm brown)
#1A1A1A — Eyes (near-black, almost no iris visible)
#FFB93066 — Neck mark (amber, semi-transparent — hard to see)

Animation Notes

MOVEMENT

Never hurries. Every movement purposeful, economical, slightly slower than the scene's emotional urgency demands. This should make audiences feel slightly off-rhythm in his scenes — like the tempo is wrong.

BLINK RATE

Approximately 60% of normal human blink frequency. Not robotic — just slightly wrong. Most viewers won't consciously register it. Their discomfort will register.

THE NECK MARK REVEAL

Chapter 2, single frame: low-angle shot as he looks down at his tablet. His collar shifts 2cm. The mark catches the operations center light for exactly one second. This is the only hint. Don't linger. Don't underscore it musically. Trust the audience.

DESIGN SHEET · DS-006
REYES
AGENCY OFFICER · MERIDIAN FIELD OPS · NON-RESONANT
AGE: 33 / HEIGHT: 5'11"
BUILD: AVERAGE · PRACTICAL · NOT MEMORABLE
HAIR: DARK BROWN · SHORT · FIELD-CUT
EYES: BROWN · EXPRESSIVE (HE TRIES TO HIDE THIS)
SKIN: MEDIUM OLIVE · FAINT SCAR: LEFT JAW (OLD)

Silhouette Design Brief

Reyes is designed to be forgettable at first — and then, slowly, to become someone the audience can't stop watching. Average height, average build, nondescript Meridian field gear. He's supposed to be part of the institutional background. That's what makes his gradual emergence as an individual feel meaningful.

The key design element: his eyes betray him. Reyes has the face of someone trying to maintain professional composure and the eyes of someone losing the argument with themselves about whether they should. Every time we see him, the gap between his composed expression and his expressive eyes widens slightly. The audience tracks it before the other characters do.

Costume — Base Design

  • Meridian Field Operations standard kit — well-maintained, correctly worn. He follows dress code.
  • Off-duty (Chapter 2 surveillance): civilian clothes that look like a Field Officer trying to look like a civilian. Close, but not quite right. Kairo clocks it immediately.
  • Badge on inside of jacket, not outside. Force of habit.
  • One personal item visible: a worn photograph tucked in his front chest pocket. Never identified. Never explained. Always there.

Color Palette

#2A3040 — Field kit (muted corporate blue-grey)
#C4955A — Skin (medium olive warm)
#6B4A2A — Eyes (warm dark brown)
#E8D4A0 — Photograph paper (worn, yellowing)

Animation Notes

EYES VS FACE

The key acting challenge for Reyes in every scene: his face says "professional following protocol." His eyes say "I'm not sure about this anymore." The dissonance should grow across episodes.

THE PHOTOGRAPH

Never show it clearly. It's visible in his chest pocket in every scene but always partially obscured. This is intentional mystery. It's load-bearing for a later arc. Don't resolve it in Season 1.

KAIRO INTERACTION

Whenever Reyes is in a scene with Kairo, Reyes should appear slightly more self-conscious than usual — Kairo's calm makes Reyes's institutional performance feel more visible, including to Reyes himself.

DESIGN SHEET · DS-007
MARDA
THE COORDINATOR · SUPPRESSED RESONANT · FRACTURE EMPATHY
AGE: 54 / HEIGHT: 5'6"
BUILD: SOLID · BUILT FOR ENDURANCE
HAIR: GREY-STREAKED BLACK · SHORT · PRACTICAL
EYES: DARK BROWN · READS EVERYTHING
SKIN: DEEP BROWN · WEATHERED · ASHFIELD-WORN

Silhouette Design Brief

Marda is the visual anchor of the group — steady, grounded, the body language of someone who has been in the Ashfield long enough to move through it like it's an extension of herself. She is the oldest character in the core cast by twenty years and this should be visible: not weakness, not diminishment, but the specific physical authority that comes from being the most experienced person in any environment you put her in.

Her design should immediately communicate: this person has been doing this longer than anyone. Everything she wears is functional and worn-in. She has no time for anything that isn't useful. Her face is the face of someone who has delivered hard news with grace and celebrated small victories with genuine feeling — someone who has lived a full life in a place that makes full lives difficult.

Costume — Base Design

  • Layered practical field clothing — Ashfield-manufactured, not corporate. Repaired multiple times with visible, competent mending.
  • Coordinator's comms unit: old hardware, analog, always on her belt. The model Kairo uses is the same brand — she gave him his first unit when he was sixteen.
  • Boots that have been re-soled twice. She's had them for twelve years.
  • A pendant worn under her shirt — never shown fully. Something from before. Pre-fracture. Non-negotiable character detail.
  • Reading glasses in a front pocket. She doesn't always use them. She sometimes puts them on to buy time to think.

Color Palette

#2D2418 — Outer layer (deep warm brown)
#4A3828 — Mid layer (worn tobacco brown)
#5A3A2A — Skin (deep warm brown, Ashfield-weathered)
#C8B890 — Hair grey streaks (warm silver)
#FF2D5540 — Subtle ability glow when active (suppressed red)

Ability Visual Language

Fracture Empathy: Twenty years suppressed, so the visual language is minimal and intimate. When Marda actively reads a Void environment, she goes still and her head tilts slightly — listening to something. Her hands, if she's near a fracture surface, trace patterns unconsciously. The only visual tell: her eyes gain the faintest red iridescence at the edges — not Echo teal, not amber, but the deep red of something that has been held down for a very long time.

Post-Reveal: Once she stops suppressing (Volume 2), the ability is more visible but still subtle compared to the others — a resonance in the air around her when she's in fracture environments, the way the Echo light bends slightly toward her. She's never fought. She's never needed to. Her ability is not combat-oriented and this is a deliberate choice. She's dangerous in every other way.

ANIMATION KEY

Marda's scenes should feel different from the younger characters' scenes — steadier camera, fewer dynamic angles, more weight in stillness. She's the one who has already processed the fear. Let her convey that.

DESIGN SHEET · DS-008
SABLE
THE BETWEEN · VOIDBORN PARTIAL · LEFT SIDE CONVERTED
AGE: 24 / HEIGHT: 5'9"
BUILD: ATHLETIC · ASYMMETRIC
HAIR: BLACK · SHAVED CLOSE ON LEFT · LONGER ON RIGHT
EYES: RIGHT — DARK BROWN · LEFT — FULL AMBER
SKIN: LIGHT WARM / LEFT SIDE: AMBER-IRIDESCENT PATTERNS

Silhouette Design Brief

Sable's design is built around visible asymmetry — the defining visual principle of their existence. The left side and the right side should read as slightly different from each other even in silhouette. Hair shorter on the left. Clothing worn differently on each side — not by choice, because the left side doesn't hold fabric the same way. The left hand visible more than the right — it's the part of them that is always slightly more real, or differently real, than the rest.

The wrongness of the left side should be visible but not grotesque. It's the wrongness of something that operates on different rules in the same body — a left arm that swings slightly late, a left shoulder that sits at a marginally different angle, a left eye that catches light when the right doesn't. The audience's eyes will want to slide off it. That's correct.

Costume — Base Design

  • Meridian field gear (stripped of insignia post-defection) — functional, dark, fitted on the right side. The left side: always slightly ill-fitting because fabric doesn't drape over the converted geometry correctly.
  • Left arm often uncovered — not for display, because sleeves don't stay down properly on the left. The amber-iridescent absorption patterns on the left arm are always visible.
  • No jewelry. Nothing on the left side that could be disrupted by phasing.
  • Post-defection: Luca gives them a scarf that becomes a visual motif — worn asymmetrically, right side only. A small, deliberate act of choosing what the right side carries.

Color Palette

#1E2230 — Right side base (standard dark)
#FFB93044 — Left side overlay (amber iridescent translucent)
#C4855A — Skin right side (warm medium)
#FFB930 — Left eye / active phase (full amber)
#FFF8E8 — Integration flash (warm white when static stops)

Ability Visual Language

Phase Shift (Left Side): When the left side phases through matter, it becomes partially translucent — not invisible, but visually present in a way that matter normally isn't. The amber-iridescent patterns on the skin flare to full brightness. Objects passing through the phased left arm show distortion at the intersection point — reality briefly rendered in two versions simultaneously.

The Static: The visual representation of Sable's internal conflict — both sides fighting each other — manifests as a micro-vibration between the right and left sides. Almost imperceptible normally. Visible during stress as the left side's positioning going fractionally in and out of sync with the right. The audience should unconsciously track this as a tension indicator.

Integration Moment: When the static stops (during Orin's touch; during the Volume 1 ending) both sides go completely still simultaneously. The amber patterns dim to a soft pulse. For three seconds or three minutes, Sable looks entirely like one person. This should be beautiful.

VOLUME 2 NOTE

Post-integration, Sable's visual language shifts to full amber iridescence on both sides, synchronized. The wrongness resolves into something genuinely new — not fixed, not normal, but intentionally unified. Their design in Volume 2 should feel like the asymmetry finally making sense.
